# ScrambleIQ
## Target User Profile

### Overview

ScrambleIQ is designed primarily for grappling coaches who analyze match footage in order to improve athlete performance. The system converts uploaded match videos into structured movement data, simplified 3D replay, and AI-assisted coaching analysis.

While athletes may also benefit from the platform, the first version of ScrambleIQ is intentionally optimized for coaching workflows.

---

## Primary User: Grappling Coaches

### Role

Grappling coaches review match footage to identify technical strengths, mistakes, and areas for improvement. They provide feedback to athletes based on observed movement patterns, positional decisions, and timing during exchanges.

ScrambleIQ is intended to support this review process by highlighting key moments within a match and presenting movement data in a structured format.

---

### Typical Responsibilities

Coaches using ScrambleIQ may perform tasks such as:

- reviewing athlete competition footage
- identifying positional mistakes
- analyzing scrambles and transitions
- studying opponent tendencies
- preparing coaching feedback
- reviewing matches with athletes during training sessions

The platform should allow coaches to perform these tasks more efficiently than manual video review.

---

### Experience Level

The system should support coaches across a range of experience levels.

Typical users may include:

- beginner coaches working with local athletes
- intermediate coaches managing gym competitors
- advanced coaches preparing athletes for tournaments

The interface should remain accessible for less experienced coaches while still providing useful analytical tools for advanced practitioners.

---

### Technical Familiarity

Most users will have limited technical experience with machine learning or motion tracking systems.

Users are expected to be comfortable with:

- uploading video files
- navigating playback controls
- reviewing visual overlays
- reading analysis summaries

The interface should present advanced analysis features in a way that does not require technical knowledge of the underlying systems.

---

## Secondary User: Athletes

### Role

Athletes may use ScrambleIQ to review their own matches in order to understand mistakes, improve decision-making, and study positional exchanges.

While the system is designed primarily for coaches, athletes can benefit from reviewing analysis results and visualizations generated by the platform.

---

### Typical Use Cases

Athletes may use ScrambleIQ to:

- review competition matches
- study positional mistakes
- observe joint positioning during exchanges
- analyze scrambles and transitions
- understand coaching feedback tied to specific timestamps

---

### Experience Level

Athletes using ScrambleIQ may range from beginner practitioners to experienced competitors.

The interface should therefore support:

- simple viewing and playback
- clear analysis summaries
- visual cues for important moments in a match

---

## Skill Level Range

ScrambleIQ is intended to support grappling practitioners across multiple experience levels.

These may include:

- beginners learning fundamental positions
- intermediate competitors refining technique
- advanced athletes preparing for competition

The system should not assume expert-level knowledge from the user.

At the same time, the analysis produced should remain valuable for experienced coaches and competitors.

---

## Environment of Use

ScrambleIQ will typically be used in the following environments:

- gyms reviewing training footage
- coaches analyzing competition matches
- athletes reviewing their own performance
- coaching sessions where match footage is discussed

The system must therefore support both individual review and collaborative coaching discussions.

---

## User Goals

Users of ScrambleIQ are generally trying to achieve the following goals:

1. Identify mistakes in grappling exchanges.
2. Understand positional transitions during a match.
3. Review scrambles and movement sequences.
4. Extract meaningful insights from match footage.
5. Reduce the time required to analyze matches manually.

---

## Summary

ScrambleIQ is designed primarily for grappling coaches who need a faster and more structured way to analyze match footage.

By converting video into joint tracking data, simplified 3D replay, and AI-generated coaching observations, the system aims to assist coaches and athletes in reviewing matches more efficiently and identifying meaningful technical insights.
